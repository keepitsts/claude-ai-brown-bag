# Simple Technology Solutions: a pre-presentation briefing

**Simple Technology Solutions, Inc. (STS) is a fast-growing, minority-owned federal IT contractor headquartered in Washington, D.C., specializing in cloud engineering, AI/ML, and data services for U.S. law enforcement and public safety missions.** Founded in late 2014 by Adrian Rich — a former special education teacher turned GovCon executive — the company has grown from a small consultancy to roughly 110 employees and recently landed a landmark **$60 million Department of the Interior contract**. STS holds rare dual 8(a) and HUBZone certifications alongside advanced cloud partnerships with AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure, a combination the company claims is unique among federal small businesses. For your presentation, understand that you're speaking to a mission-driven, technically sophisticated team with deep federal law enforcement expertise and a strong people-first culture.

---

## What STS does and who they serve

STS brands itself under the tagline **"Digital Solutions for National Security Missions."** The company's core business is modernizing technology for federal civilian agencies, with a heavy concentration in the Department of Homeland Security ecosystem (USCIS, CBP, ICE) and, more recently, the Department of the Interior's law enforcement programs.

Their five core capability areas are:

- **Cloud & Site Reliability Engineering** — multi-cloud infrastructure design, DevSecOps automation, Infrastructure-as-Code, and AIOps across AWS, Azure, and GCP
- **Data Engineering, AI & Machine Learning (DEAM)** — enterprise data platforms, entity resolution, NLP pipelines, document intelligence, and predictive analytics
- **Application Modernization** — migrating legacy systems including mainframe/COBOL applications to cloud-native architectures using CI/CD, microservices, and low-code platforms
- **Cloud Contact Center-as-a-Service** — AI-enhanced Amazon Connect deployments for federal agencies
- **Law Enforcement Technology** — records management, digital evidence discovery, and agentic AI solutions for officer compliance

STS has also developed several proprietary products: **A³ (Triple-A)**, a federal-compliant headless AI platform with pre-integrated LLM and computer vision services; **C-CAT**, a generative AI tool that automates CJIS security policy reviews (reducing **50+ minute manual reviews to under 4 minutes**); and **Law Enforcement Inspector (LEI)**, an agentic AI solution that automates incident notifications and compliance reporting for officers. These products signal a company actively transitioning from pure services to a hybrid services-plus-products model.

---

## The $60M DOI contract that changed STS's trajectory

The most significant recent development is STS's **August 2024 win of a $60 million single-award IDIQ** with the Department of the Interior, beating seven other bidders. Partnering with public safety platform provider Mark43, STS deployed a cloud-native Law Enforcement Records Management System (LERMS) and Computer Aided Dispatch (CAD) across **six DOI law enforcement programs, approximately 3,000 sworn officers, and 855+ locations nationwide**. The system went live in October 2025 after just 13 months of development — covering roughly 20% of U.S. landmass and 40% of the southern border.

This contract represents a major scaling milestone. Other notable contracts include a **$110 million OPM Data Science & Analytics BPA** (through their joint venture "Applied Cloud"), a **$4.5 million DHS/USCIS contact center** engagement, and NASA's Earth Observing System Data and Information System (EOSDIS) work. STS also recently won a DOI OCIO Artificial Intelligence Support Services contract, further cementing their federal AI positioning.

## Culture, values, and what makes STS tick

STS's culture is genuinely distinctive in the GovCon space, and understanding it will help you connect with your audience. Three themes dominate.

**First, "Techquity" — their coined term blending technology and equity.** CEO Adrian Rich has stated: "For us, DEIA is not a fad or an initiative — it is who we are." The company runs apprenticeship programs creating career on-ramps in cloud, DevOps, data science, and low-code development for individuals from underserved communities, particularly Black and brown communities. They partnered with Unqork to offer no-code certification programs. This isn't just branding — STS is a certified minority-owned, 8(a), HUBZone small disadvantaged business, and they offer a **$5,000 annual bonus** for employees living in qualified HUBZone areas.

**Second, their "90% of 100%" philosophy.** Employees are expected to work at 90% capacity, with the remaining 10% reserved for growth, development, mentoring, and self-care. Combined with unlimited PTO (with leadership actively encouraging time off), remote work flexibility, and engagement activities like virtual escape rooms, this produces strong employee satisfaction. STS has earned **Washington Business Journal "Best Places to Work" honors three times** (2019, 2021, 2023) and maintains a **4.4/5 Glassdoor rating** with 85% of employees recommending the company.

**Third, their mission-first orientation.** The company explicitly states its goal as "singular — to advance missions that matter." The About page emphasizes treating data as the primary asset to solve hard problems for law enforcement and public safety. This is not a company motivated primarily by contract revenue — they genuinely identify with their federal customers' missions.

## Size, certifications, and competitive positioning

STS occupies an interesting niche: small enough to leverage set-aside contracting advantages, but increasingly capable of competing for — and winning — large contracts. PitchBook lists **110 employees** (LinkedIn says 51–200). The company has raised approximately **$445K** in total funding, suggesting primarily organic, revenue-funded growth.

Their certification stack is formidable for a company this size. **CMMI Level 3** (Development and Services) demonstrates process maturity. A recently awarded **Top Secret Facility Security Clearance** from DCSA enables classified work. **ISO 9001** certification covers quality management. And their **AWS Advanced Consulting Partner** status — combined with Google Cloud and Azure advanced partnerships — positions them as a credible multi-cloud provider.

Growth has been rapid: STS appeared on the **Inc. 5000 three consecutive years** (#429 in 2019 with 453% three-year growth, #549 in 2020, #1070 in 2021) and ranked **#13 on Washington Technology's Fast 50** in 2019. They've also been recognized as an OrangeSlices AI Elev8 GovCon honoree for four consecutive years through 2026.

Contract vehicles include **GSA STARS III**, **GSA MAS IT & Cloud Services**, and **DHS TACCOM II**, giving them broad access to federal procurement channels.

## Conclusion: what to know walking into the room

You're presenting to a technically sharp, mission-oriented team that cares deeply about both outcomes and values. Five things to keep in mind. **They think in terms of federal compliance constraints** — FedRAMP, ATO, CJIS, and data sharing across jurisdictions are daily realities, not abstractions. **They're in a growth inflection point** — the $60M DOI win and Top Secret clearance represent a step-change in scale. **AI is their current strategic bet** — products like A³, C-CAT, and LEI show they're investing heavily in applied AI for federal missions. **Culture and diversity aren't talking points but core identity** — Techquity, apprenticeship programs, and the founder's background make this genuine. **They value simplicity and pragmatism** — their brand, their blog ("Keep IT Simple"), and their stated philosophy all emphasize making complex technology accessible and mission-ready rather than impressive for its own sake. Match that energy in your presentation.
